# Inspire VST - Enhanced UX Features Guide

## What's New? 🎵

Your Inspire VST plugin just got a major UX upgrade! Here's what you'll notice when you use it:

---

## 1. Producer Lab & Editor Suite Now Work! 🚀

### Before
- Producer Lab and Editor Suite showed empty screens
- No pack generation in these modes
- Thought they were broken

### Now ✅
- **Producer Lab** displays music production packs with:
  - Available submode choices (sampler, musician, sound-designer)
  - Full pack generation workflow
  - Sample preview, save, and DAW insertion
  
- **Editor Suite** displays visual editing packs with:
  - Available submode choices (image-editor, video-editor, audio-editor)
  - Pack generation with visual/editing content
  - Integration with your editing workflow

**How to use:**
1. Create/join a room in Inspire VST
2. Click the **Producer Lab** or **Editor Suite** card
3. You'll see available packs and a "Generate Pack" button
4. Click to create new packs with AI-suggested content

---

## 2. Filter Your Content Before Generating 🎚️

### New Feature: Relevance Filters

Now you can fine-tune exactly what kind of content you want:

**Three Filter Dimensions:**

#### Timeframe: How Current?
- **Fresh** - Latest trends from this week (trending sounds, current events)
- **Recent** - Recent content from the past month (seasonal vibes, current mood)
- **Timeless** - Classic, evergreen content (timeless melodies, universal themes)

#### Tone: What Vibe?
- **Funny** 🎪 - Playful, humorous, lighthearted content
- **Deep** 🎭 - Emotional, reflective, introspective content
- **Dark** 🌑 - Edgy, gritty, darker aesthetic content

#### Semantic Distance: How Experimental?
- **Tight** 🎯 - Focused, precise suggestions (keep it close to your theme)
- **Balanced** ⚖️ - Diverse mix of ideas (balanced exploration)
- **Wild** 🌀 - Experimental, unexpected suggestions (push boundaries)

**Where are they?**
- Located above the pack list in Writer Lab, Producer Lab, and Editor Suite
- Cyan-colored buttons grouped by category
- Click any button to select that option

**How to use:**
1. Select the filters that match your current mood/project
2. Click "Generate Pack" to create content matching those filters
3. Change filters anytime to explore different creative directions

---

## 3. Remix Button - Quick Pack Variations 🔄

### New Feature: One-Click Remix

The **"♻️ Remix Pack"** button lets you quickly generate variations.

**What it does:**
- Keeps your current filters the same
- Generates completely new content
- Great for when you like the vibe but want fresh options

**Where is it?**
- Next to the "Generate Pack" button
- Green-colored button (♻️ icon)
- Works in Writer Lab, Producer Lab, and Editor Suite

**How to use:**
1. Generate a pack or select a pack from the list
2. Look at the pack detail on the right
3. Like the vibe but want something different?
4. Click **"♻️ Remix Pack"**
5. New pack appears instantly with same filters, different content!

**Pro Tip:** 
- Use Tight filters + Remix to explore variations on a theme
- Use Wild filters + Remix to push into unexpected territories

## How to Get the Most Out Of It

### Workflow: Discovery + Remix + Refine

1. **Discovery Phase**
   - Set filters to "Wild" or "Balanced" to explore
   - Generate multiple packs
   - Get inspired by variety

2. **Refinement Phase** 
   - Find a pack you like the vibe of
   - Set filters to "Tight" to narrow scope
   - Click Remix to get similar-but-different content
   - Save packs to your library

3. **Production Phase**
   - Use saved packs as starting points
   - Remix and experiment
   - Insert into DAW when ready

### Filter Combinations to Try

**For Brainstorming:**
- Timeframe: Fresh
- Tone: Any (try all three!)
- Semantic: Wild

**For Focused Work:**
- Timeframe: Recent
- Tone: Match your mood
- Semantic: Tight

**For Daily Inspiration:**
- Timeframe: Timeless
- Tone: Deep
- Semantic: Balanced

## Troubleshooting

**Q: Producer Lab shows empty list**
- Make sure you're in a room (Create/Join Room first)
- Click "Generate Pack" to create your first pack

**Q: Filters don't seem to be working**
- Check the filter buttons - active ones are bright cyan
- Click a button to change filters
- Generate a new pack to see filter effects

**Q: Remix button is greyed out**
- First, select or generate a pack
- The remix button enables once you have a pack to work with

**Q: Why is Filter X selected by default?**
- Default: Fresh, Funny, Tight (balanced exploration)
- You can change anytime - filters persist for your session
